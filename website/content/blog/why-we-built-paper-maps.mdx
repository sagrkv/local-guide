Every map app shows you everything. Every restaurant, every shop, every ATM. Thousands of pins fighting for your attention, ranked by an algorithm that rewards popularity over quality.

We wanted the opposite.

Paper Maps shows you 50 places, not 50,000. Each one chosen by a person who's been there — who knows that the dosa at Mylari is better than the one across the street, that Chamundi Hill is best at sunrise, that the real Devaraja Market experience is in the flower section at 7 AM.

No star ratings. No user reviews. No sponsored listings. If it's on the map, it's good. That's the whole point.

We use AI to help us discover candidate places and draft descriptions. But a human curator reviews and approves every single thing you see. No algorithm decides what makes the cut — a person does.

Paper Maps is free. Beautiful city guides shouldn't be behind a paywall. We're a small studio called summar studios, and we make this because we love cities.

If Paper Maps helps you find one place you wouldn't have found otherwise, it was worth building.
